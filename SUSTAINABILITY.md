# Sustainability & Economic Strategy

## Funding Models
The BOSC Community Library aims for long-term viability by leveraging a hybrid economic model.

### 1. Service-Based Model (The "Red Hat" Approach)
In this model, the BOSC core remains free, but revenue is generated through:
- **Custom Integration**: Helping local governments integrate the library with existing legacy systems.
- **Support Contracts**: SLAs (Service Level Agreements) for critical educational infrastructure.
- **Certification**: Training local IT professionals to manage and deploy the library.

### 2. Foundation-Driven Model
This approach involves establishing a "BOSC Foundation" to:
- **Grant Management**: Securing funding from international educational bodies (e.g., UNESCO).
- **Stewardship**: Ensuring the neutral governance of the code and preventing vendor lock-in.
- **Infrastructure**: Hosting a central, verified resource hub for the community.

## Comparative Analysis
| Model | Scalability | Local Impact | Stability |
|-------|-------------|--------------|-----------|
| **Red Hat** | High (Commercial drive) | Strong (Local jobs) | Dependent on market |
| **Foundation** | Medium (Grant-based) | High (Mission-driven) | High (Long-term focus) |

For the BOSC Community Library, we recommend a **Foundation-led model with a vibrant service ecosystem**. This ensures the public interest is always prioritized while allowing local entrepreneurs to build businesses around the platform.
