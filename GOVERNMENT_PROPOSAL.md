# Formal Proposal: Adoption of BOSC Community Library

**To:** The Ministry of Education
**From:** Lead Community Maintainer, BOSC project
**Subject:** Transitioning to Open Source Educational Infrastructure

## Executive Summary
We propose the adoption of the **BOSC Community Library** as the national standard for digital resource management. This move will eliminate vendor lock-in, reduce Total Cost of Ownership (TCO), and empower local communities.

## The Problem with Proprietary Alternatives
Current proprietary solutions (e.g., [Company X]) impose significant financial burdens through:
- **High Licensing Fees**: Recurring costs that do not build local capacity.
- **Vendor Lock-in**: Data is stored in "walled gardens," making it difficult to migrate or integrate with local tools.
- **Lack of Transparency**: Inability to audit security or customize the software for local languages (e.g., Swahili).

## The BOSC Advantage
1. **Zero License Fees**: Redirect budget from software licenses to teacher training and hardware.
2. **Total Cost of Ownership (TCO)**: While open source requires maintenance, the TCO is significantly lower over 5 years as the Ministry gains autonomy and avoids "upgrade traps."
3. **Sovereignty**: The Ministry owns the infrastructure. The code is audited locally, and metadata is stored in standard formats.
4. **Community Localization**: Built-in support for localized metadata ensures no student is left behind due to language barriers.

## Recommendation
We recommend a pilot program in 10 regional libraries to demonstrate the efficiency and resilience of the BOSC Community Library before a nationwide rollout.
